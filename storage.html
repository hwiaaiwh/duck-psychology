Storage

Step 1: Sensory Memory

The initial memories that are generated from sensory information.

  • Held for only a fraction of a second.
  • Iconic: a momentary recording of a visual stimuli.
    • Ex. Seeing an afterimage of a face right after briefly looking at it.
  • Echoic: a momentary recording of an auditory stimuli.
    • Ex. Hearing a person's voice after they're done talking.

Step 2: Short Term Memory

The memories that can be recalled temporarily after encoding.

  • Held for only 10-20 seconds.
  • Can hold 5-9 items at once.
  • Brain tries to figure out if it's "useful or not".
